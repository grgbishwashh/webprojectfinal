Popular Sports of the World

Cricket

Rules

  1. Two teams with 11 players each.
  2. Toss: Coin toss decides who bats/bowls first.
  3. Innings: Both teams bat and bowl.
  4. Batting: Score runs by hitting and running.
  5. Bowling/Fielding: Dismiss batsmen, prevent runs.
  6. Wickets: Hit stumps to get batsmen out.
  7. Runs: Score by running or hitting boundaries.
  8. Extras: Additional runs (no-balls, wides, etc.).
  9. Overs: Six legal deliveries by one bowler.
  10. Powerplays: Fielding restrictions in limited-overs.
  11. Declaring Inning: Optional in longer formats.
  12. Winning: Team with most runs wins.

Football

Rules

  1. Objective: Score goals by getting the ball into the opponent's net.
  2. Team: Each team has 11 players, including a goalkeeper.
  3. Duration: A standard match consists of two halves, each lasting 45 minutes.
  4. Offside: Players cannot be closer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last defender when the ball is played to them.
  5. Fouls: Players cannot use excessive force, make dangerous tackles, or engage in unsporting behavior. Fouls result in free kicks or penalties.
  6. Yellow and Red Cards: Referees use yellow cards to caution players for misconduct and red cards for more serious offenses, leading to ejection.
  7. Throw-ins: When the ball goes out of play along the sidelines, the opposing team is awarded a throw-in.
  8. Goal Kicks: When the attacking team kicks the ball out over the defensive team's goal line, the defending team is awarded a goal kick.
  9. Corner Kicks: When the defending team kicks the ball out over their own goal line, the attacking team is awarded a corner kick.
  10. Penalty Kicks: Awarded for serious fouls committed within the penalty area, providing a one-on-one opportunity for the attacking player and the goalkeeper.
  11. Substitutions: Each team is allowed a specific number of substitutions during a match.

Basketball

Rules

  1. Teams: Each team has five players on the court at a time.
  2. Scoring: Points are scored by shooting the ball through the opponent's basket. A field goal from beyond the three-point arc is worth three points, and a shot inside the arc is worth two points. Free throws, awarded after certain fouls, are worth one point each.
  3. Game Duration: A standard basketball game consists of four quarters, each lasting 12 minutes (NBA) or 10 minutes (FIBA/international play).
  4. Possession and Shot Clock: Teams alternate possession of the ball, and they have a limited time (shot clock) to attempt a shot (usually 24 seconds in the NBA and 14 seconds in FIBA).
  5. Dribbling: Players must dribble the ball (bounce it on the floor) while moving. Double dribbling (starting to dribble again after stopping) is not allowed.
  6. Violations: Common violations include traveling (taking too many steps without dribbling), double dribble, carrying/palming the ball, and goaltending (blocking a shot on its way down).
  7. Fouls: Personal fouls are called for illegal physical contact. Teams enter the bonus situation after a certain number of team fouls, leading to free throws for the opposing team.
  8. Free Throws: Awarded for certain fouls. Players shoot from a designated free-throw line, and each successful shot is worth one point.
  9. Substitutions: Teams can substitute players during stoppages in play.
  10. Jump Ball: Used to start the game and determine possession after certain stoppages.
  11. Backcourt Violation: Once a team advances the ball past the half-court line, they cannot move it back into their own backcourt.
  12. Timeouts: Each team is allowed a certain number of timeouts during a game.

Table Tennis

Rules

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, has specific rules governing its play. Here's a summary of some basic table tennis rules:

  1. Scoring: A game is played to 11 points, and a player must win by at least two points. Matches are usually best of 5 or 7 games.
  2. Serve: The ball must be thrown vertically at least 6 inches before being struck. The server must hit the ball on their side of the table, and it must bounce once on their side before crossing the net.
  3. Service Rotation: In singles, each player serves two points in a row, and in doubles, each team serves two points in a row. The serve alternates between opponents or teams.
  4. Ball in Play: The ball must be struck so that it bounces once on each side of the table. If the ball hits the net and goes over during a serve, it's a let, and the serve is retaken.
  5. Scoring during Play: A point is scored if the opponent fails to return the ball within the rules. Points can also be won if the opponent commits certain infractions, such as hitting the ball twice in succession.
  6. Change of Ends: Players switch ends of the table after every odd-numbered game in a match.
  7. Continuous Play: After each point, the server quickly retrieves the ball and serves again. There are no breaks between points, except for a 1-minute break when one player or team reaches 5 points in the deciding game.
  8. Let: A let is called if a point is interrupted or a service is disrupted for some reason. The point or serve is replayed.
  9. Equipment: Players use a table tennis racket (paddle or bat) to hit the ball. The ball must be made of celluloid or a similar plastic material.
  10. Doubles Play: In doubles, players on the same team alternate hitting the ball. The ball must bounce on each side of the table during play.
